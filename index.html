Visualizing African American Social Mobility In The US In The Past 100 Years

This website will help users visualize trends of indicators of African American social mobility in the US in the last 100 years. The data was downloaded and scraped from IPUMS US, which is a website and database providing access to over sixty integrated, high-precision samples of the American population drawn from federal censuses. The data was then processed through R code in Rstudio and used to create the visualizations below. Note that all the data below only shows adults from ages 25-64 and data was used starting from roughly 100 years ago to the present time.

Some of the key indicators used in this project to indicate social mobility included socioeconomic status, income, and education level. These three key indicators were quantitatively specified by SEI, OCCSCORE, and EDUC from IPUMS US. SEI or the Duncan Socioeconmic Index is a score assigned to each occupation using the 1950 occupational classification scheme available in the OCC1950 variable, and is a measure of occupational status based upon the income level and educational attainment associated with each occupation in 1950. OCCSCORE was measured by assigning each occuptation a value representing the median total income, in hundreds of 1950s dollars, of all people in that occupation in 1950. EDUC was measured by a number from 00 to 11 where the number corresponded to roughly the level of education of an individual, where 00 represents no schooling and 11 represents 5+ years of college.
